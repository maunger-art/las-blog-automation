Privacy Policy

Last updated: 17 March 2026

1. Who we are

Layer Advisory Services is operated by Erica Layer, a sole trader based in the United Kingdom. You can contact us at: erica@layeradvisory.com

2. What this policy covers

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect personal data when you visit layeradvisory.com and blog.layeradvisory.com. We comply with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

3. Data we collect

3.1 Contact form submissions

When you submit a contact form we collect your name, email address, and message content. Legal basis: Legitimate interests. Retention: up to 24 months.

3.2 Blog analytics

We collect anonymised data about how visitors use our blog — pages visited, time on page, referral sources. This data does not identify individual visitors. Legal basis: Legitimate interests.

3.3 Calendly booking data

If you book a call via Calendly, they collect your name and email. See Calendly's Privacy Policy at calendly.com/privacy. We use booking details solely to conduct our call.

4. How we use your data

We use your data to respond to enquiries, prepare for calls, improve our websites, and comply with legal obligations. We do not use automated decision-making or profiling.

5. How we share your data

We do not sell or rent your data. Trusted third-party providers include Calendly (scheduling), GitHub Pages (blog hosting), and Anthropic (content generation — no personal data is passed to the API).

6. International transfers

Some providers are based outside the UK. We ensure appropriate safeguards including standard contractual clauses approved by the ICO.

7. Data security

We use HTTPS encryption and restrict access to personal data. No internet transmission is fully secure, but we take every reasonable precaution.

8. Your rights under UK GDPR

You have the right to access, rectify, erase, restrict, or port your data, and to object to processing. To exercise any right, contact erica@layeradvisory.com. You may also complain to the ICO at ico.org.uk.

9. Cookies

We use minimal cookies — no advertising or tracking cookies. Any cookies are strictly necessary or used for anonymised analytics only.

10. Children's privacy

Our services are for business professionals. We do not knowingly collect data from children under 16.

11. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy periodically. The last updated date above reflects any changes.
